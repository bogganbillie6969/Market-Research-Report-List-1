<p><p>Calcium sulphate, commonly known as gypsum, is a naturally occurring mineral composed of calcium, sulfur, and oxygen. It exists in various forms, including dihydrate (CaSO₄·2H₂O), hemihydrate (CaSO₄·0.5H₂O), and anhydrite (CaSO₄). It is widely used in industries such as construction, agriculture, food, pharmaceuticals, and chemicals. In construction, it is a key component in plaster, drywall, and cement. In agriculture, it serves as a soil conditioner and fertilizer. In food, it acts as a coagulant and firming agent, while in pharmaceuticals, it is used as an excipient.</p><p>The Calcium Sulphate Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.3% during the forecast period. The rising awareness of sustainable building materials and the benefits of gypsum in soil improvement further contribute to market expansion. Additionally, advancements in production technologies and the development of high-purity calcium sulphate for specialized applications are key trends shaping the market. However, environmental concerns related to mining and processing may pose challenges. Overall, the market is poised for steady growth, supported by its versatile applications and increasing industrial demand.</p></p>
